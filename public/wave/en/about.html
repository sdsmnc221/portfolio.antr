The Project

One of the main sources of income of the province of Ba Ria in Southern Vietnam belongs to fish and seafood farming near the sea. However, recently, due to an aggravated water pollution as the result of human activities, the province has to face a difficult situation of decreasing seafood farming products.

In order to bring in help, to save fishes and seafoods from the pollution, the WAVE project was launched. Being carried out under the partnership between UBO, school group of La Croix Rouge La Salle Brest (France) and the University of Ba Ria - Vung Tau (Vietnam), the project consists of making a wireless monitoring system of water parameters, dedicated to Vietnamese aquaculture. This is a rewarding and multidisciplinary scientific project, involving electronics, computer sciences, mechanism, automatism, as well as chemistry and biologie.

This project, lasting for two academic years, from 2016 to 2018, was being carried out in 2017 by students of BTS Systèmes numériques (BTS SN), and is continued by students of Licence professionnelle Systèmes d’Electronique Marine Embarqués (LP SEME), under directions of Mr. Claude VO, professor of BTS SN and LP SEME, as well as of Mr. Philippe TALBOT, director of the the same Licence.

The System

The first pump pumps seawater into the measuring tank for analysis purposes. Once done, the measured data is sent to the electronic control box by sensors, via radio wave or GSM, then updated into the database and accessed via a Human Machine Interface (HIM, in our case, a web interface) and a web server.

The electronic valve then empty the measuring tank, so that the second pump can fill it with fresh water from the water tank. The motor is also activated in order to clean the sensors. Once done, the valve empty again the measuring tank, which is once more ready for the measuring and analysing processes. This monitoring cycle can be automated, and its frequency can be easily configured.

TThe system is supplied by solar energy. The electronic control box is shipped with an LCD screen and a rotary switch helping facilitate data visualisation, as well as calibration of sensors, monitoring cycle’s frequency and communication options (GSM/Radio).
